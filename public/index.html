THIS IS NOT A MUSIC WEBSITE

It is a way
of hearing.

Most listeners hear the whole. I enter one strand, remain with it, and let the rest of the texture reorganize around it. One voice becomes a world. Then another. Then the relationship between them.

THE POLYPHONIC MIND

Three ways
inside the music.

Counterpoint becomes astonishing when the voices stop sounding like a block and begin behaving like independent intelligences.

IIsolate

Pull one line out of the texture until its contour, rhythm, tension, and personality become physically familiar.

IIAnticipate

Listen again without playing. Feel the selected voice arriving before it sounds.

IIIRelate

Hear how one voice provokes, resists, shadows, supports, and transforms every other voice.

01 · THE PRACTICE

One piece.
One voice.
Deep attention.

I randomly select a work—perhaps an aria from a cantata—then focus on one randomly selected voice, such as the viola, or sometimes a “virtual” voice hidden within the texture.

Beyond structural, macro-level analysis, my real interest is in the microscopic life of the music: relationships between voices, internal patterns, and the behavior that only reveals itself through sustained listening.

I come from a background in musicology, music theory, and analysis. But this practice does not depend on academic training. Its essential tools are patience, repetition, curiosity, and attentive ears.

01Play

Isolate and perform one voice until its physical shape becomes familiar.

02Listen

Hear that voice inside the complete texture without playing along.

03Anticipate

Repeat until you can feel its next move within the polyphony.

25

Years of detailed listeningOver time, this process builds a deep, embodied understanding of counterpoint—not only how the voices are written, but how they think together.

ANALYTICAL PRINCIPLES

The score is evidence.
The ear is the inquiry.

Computational description can direct attention; it cannot replace stylistic judgment. FisoBach keeps that boundary visible.

OBSERVED

What the tool establishes

Pitch, duration, onset, register, voice entry, vertical interval, and directional motion are read directly from the entered notation.

HEURISTIC

What the tool proposes

Imitation, structural convergence, stability, and resolution are listening prompts—useful hypotheses that require the score, context, and ear.

CONTEXTUAL

What remains yours

Harmony, contrapuntal function, text, rhetoric, affect, tuning, articulation, and historical style belong to a situated musical reading.

Working principle A dissonance is not an error, a parallel is not automatically a fault, and a numerical majority is not musical significance. The lab marks events so that you can listen more precisely.
